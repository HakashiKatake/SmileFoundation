WHY HEALTH CANNOT WAIT

Last mile delivery of healthcare is always challenging in a country as vast and diverse as India. The uneven distribution of healthcare infrastructure, as well as medical professionals, makes quality healthcare services inaccessible to a large population living in rural areas. Urban slum dwellers, on the other hand, suffer from a lack of awareness, adverse living conditions, and the unwillingness to lose a day's wage in order to reach the nearest medical facility.

To address these challenges, Smile Foundation's 'Health Cannot Wait' initiative is taking quality healthcare services directly to the doorstep of vulnerable communities, with a focus on women and children. This initiative aims to strengthen the country's existing healthcare system and reduce out-of-pocket expenses on health for those who need it the most. Awareness activities like school health camps and street plays are also conducted to encourage children and communities to adopt health-seeking behaviors.

But we can't do this alone. The harsh reality is that millions of underprivileged families in India still struggle to access basic healthcare, leading to preventable illnesses and even loss of life. Your support can change that. By contributing to the 'Health Cannot Wait' initiative, you will ensure that essential medical care reaches those who need it most—right at their doorstep.

Your donation can save lives and provide a brighter, healthier future for countless women and children across the country. For the poor, health is truly their only wealth. Donate for healthcare NOW and be the difference between hope and despair. Together, we can build a healthier and more equitable India, where no one is left behind.

STORIES OF HOPE

Divyanshi, Rajasthan

A mother's heart is tied to her child's. There is no doctor in our village. The nearest clinic is 5km away and we have to walk all that way. Two weeks ago, my daughter started crying continuously. I didn't know what to do. My husband was out for work. Me and my mother in law tried our best to soothe her, but she won't settle down. Then my mother in law said today is the day that the health van will come. The van started coming to our village once every 15 days, with doctors and free medicines. When I brought him to the medical van, the doctor told me she has diarrhoea. He gave me proper treatment guidelines and packets of ORS solution which helped to calm her. The moment she started smiling again, I was finally relieved!

Ramesh, Maharashtra

I am a daily wage worker and the sole breadwinner for my family of five. Last year, I started experiencing severe pain in my chest but couldn't afford to miss work to visit a hospital. When the Smile Foundation organized a health camp in our community, I decided to get checked. The doctor diagnosed me with early-stage hypertension and immediately put me on medication. They also taught me about dietary changes and exercises. The free medicines they provided saved me from spending money I didn't have. Today, my blood pressure is under control, and I can continue working without worry. These health camps are a blessing for people like us who cannot afford to lose a day's wage.

Meena, Uttar Pradesh

During my pregnancy, I faced many complications but couldn't access proper healthcare as the nearest hospital was 20 kilometers away. The Smile Foundation's telemedicine center in our village became my lifeline. Through video consultations with gynecologists from the city hospital, I received proper care and guidance throughout my pregnancy. When it was time for delivery, they arranged emergency transport to the hospital. Today, both my son and I are healthy. Without the telemedicine facility, I don't know if we would have survived the complications. Now, I tell all expecting mothers in our village to use the telemedicine services for regular check-ups.

Arjun, Tamil Nadu

I used to miss school frequently because of recurring stomach infections. My parents are farmers and couldn't afford to take me to a private doctor. When Smile Foundation conducted a health camp at our school, the doctor identified that I had a parasitic infection due to unsafe drinking water. They provided me with medication and taught our family about water purification methods. They also installed a water filter at our school. It's been six months since then, and I haven't fallen sick. I can attend school regularly now and don't have to worry about stomach pain anymore. I want to become a doctor when I grow up and help children in my village just like the doctors who helped me.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Can I contribute to the "Health Cannot Wait" programme as an individual donor?

+

Yes, individual donors are the backbone of our "Health Cannot Wait" programme. You can contribute any amount that suits your budget, and every contribution makes a difference. We offer several convenient ways to donate - through our website, bank transfer, or even by check. Your donation will directly support healthcare services for vulnerable communities across India.

How are the healthcare projects and initiatives selected under your healthcare programme?

+

Our healthcare projects are selected based on a comprehensive needs assessment. We identify areas with limited access to healthcare facilities and high vulnerability. All projects must meet our criteria of sustainability, community involvement, and measurable impact. We also collaborate with local healthcare authorities and other stakeholders to ensure our initiatives complement existing healthcare infrastructure rather than duplicate efforts.

How will my donation to the programme be utilized?

+

Your donation will be utilized efficiently across our healthcare initiatives. Approximately 70% of funds go directly to service delivery - including mobile healthcare units, telemedicine services, medicines, and medical equipment. The remaining 30% supports essential program management, including trained healthcare professionals, community mobilization, monitoring and evaluation, and administrative costs. We maintain complete transparency and publish annual reports detailing fund utilization.

Are there any specific healthcare focus areas or target populations within the "Health Cannot Wait" programme?

+

Yes, the "Health Cannot Wait" programme has specific focus areas and target populations. We prioritize maternal and child healthcare, primary healthcare services, and preventive health education. Our target populations include rural communities with limited healthcare access, urban slum dwellers, women of reproductive age, children under 5 years, and elderly populations. However, our mobile units and camps serve everyone in need regardless of age, gender, or background.

Are my donations to this programme eligible for tax benefits?

+

Yes, all donations to the "Health Cannot Wait" programme are eligible for tax benefits under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act in India. For international donors, tax benefits may vary according to your country's regulations. We provide official receipts and 80G certificates for all donations received, which you can use for claiming tax deductions. For specific questions regarding tax benefits, we recommend consulting with your tax advisor.
